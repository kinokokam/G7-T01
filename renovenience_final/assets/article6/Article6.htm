Muji Interior Design Made Simple: Key Elements for Achieving the Look

Written By
Stephen Chan
Date Written:
11/9/2024

‍

Ever walked into the furniture section of a Muji store, and wished your house had the same aesthetic? You're not alone. The Japanese brand's minimalist aesthetic has captured hearts of Singaporeans nationwide, which is why the Muji interior design style has become largely popular in Singapore, or some might say, basic. However, at EZiD, we believe that interior design is a matter of personal taste, and if you love the appeal of the Muji interior design style,  that’s all that matters. 

Achieving the Muji look is easier than you think, and let us show you how. In this guide, let us take you through the key elements of the muji interior design style, so thatyou can bring that zen vibe into your own home. Time to grab a cup of ocha, and read on!

‍

Achieving a Muji Style Interior: Key Design Principles

From a design standpoint, it’s not hard to see why the muji interior design style is so popular—being neutral, clean and minimalist at the same time, being surrounded in this mood can evoke a feeling of calmness that no other interior design style can. Here are the key design principles:

‍

Embrace the 'less is more' philosophy

When it comes to Muji style, less really is more. Start by decluttering your space and keeping only the essentials. Think clean lines, uncluttered surfaces, and a general sense of airiness. It's all about creating a calm, peaceful environment where you can truly relax.

‍

Don't worry, though – this doesn't mean your home has to feel cold or sterile. The key is to find a balance between minimalism and comfort. Choose a few carefully selected pieces that speak to you and let them shine in your pared-back space.

‍

Opt for natural materials and neutral colours

Muji style is all about celebrating the beauty of natural materials. Wood, cotton, linen, and stone are your best friends here. Look for furniture and decor items made from these materials to create a warm, organic feel in your home.

‍

As for colours, think neutral and earthy tones. Whites, beiges, greys, and soft browns are the cornerstone of the Muji palette. These hues create a calming backdrop that's easy on the eyes and perfect for unwinding after a long day.

‍

Focus on functionality and simplicity

In true Muji fashion, every item in your home should serve a purpose. Multi-functional furniture is a great way to maximise space while keeping things minimal. Look for pieces with clean lines and simple designs – nothing too fussy or ornate.

‍

Storage is another crucial element of Muji style. Invest in clever storage solutions that help keep clutter at bay. Think built-in cabinets, sleek shelving units, and rattan boxes that blend seamlessly with your decor.

‍

Let in natural light

Natural light is a cornerstone of Muji-inspired interiors. It helps create a sense of openness and makes your space feel larger and more inviting. If possible, maximise your windows and opt for sheer curtains that let in plenty of light while still providing privacy.

‍

Can't add more windows? No worries! Strategic placement of mirrors can help bounce light around your space, creating a similar effect.

‍

By following these key principles, you'll be well on your way to creating a serene, Muji-inspired oasis in your own home. 

‍

Add Thoughtful Touches

While minimalism is key for the Muji interior design, don't forget to add a few personal touches if you don’t want your home to look too basic. At EZiD, we always recommend our homeowners to include items that reflect their personality and complement the overall aesthetic. Here are some items that can blend very well with the Muji style of design:

‍

A Carefully Curated Collection of Books

Books not only add a personal touch but also bring a sense of warmth and knowledge to your space. Opt for neutral-colored or earth-toned covers to maintain the minimalistic aesthetic.

‍

A Single Piece of Artwork

Choose one piece of artwork that resonates with you to feature in your living room. This can be a painting or even a sculpture. Not a fan of art? Don’t worry, because photographs work too! Ensure that the artwork you use complements the muted tones of the Muji design, adding character without overwhelming the simplicity.

‍

Plants to Bring a Touch of Nature Indoors

Integrating plants can enhance the serene and natural vibe of Muji interiors by actually connecting you to nature. For low-maintenance, you can choose plants like succulents, bonsai trees, or ferns. These plants not only add a splash of green but also help purify the air in your home.

Remember, in Muji-inspired design, every item should be thoughtfully chosen and purposefully placed.

‍

Design Your Muji-Inspired Home with EZiD

By following these essential tips, you will be well on your way to creating a calm, clutter-free Muji-inspired haven in your home. It's all about finding that perfect balance between simplicity and functionality– and creating a space that truly feels like home.
