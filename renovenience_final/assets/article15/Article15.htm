The Ultimate Guide to Kitchen Renovations in Singapore—Must-have Features and More

Written By
Stephen Chan
Date Written:
24/6/2024

Whether you’re a culinary enthusiast or someone who enjoys quick takeaways, your kitchen is the heart of your home. At EZiD, we understand the importance of creating a space that is not only visually appealing but also durable and highly functional. Renovating a kitchen involves many decisions, and it can be overwhelming to navigate all the options. That's why we've put together this ultimate guide to kitchen renovations in Singapore. From layouts and storage solutions to appliances and finishes, our comprehensive guide will help you plan your dream kitchen. Read on for our secret recipe to the perfect kitchen!

‍

Open vs Closed Concept

An open concept kitchen opens up your space and is perfect for entertaining and keeping an eye on little ones. However, the lack of walls means cooking smells and noise can spread to the rest of the home. These smells can actually linger for a few days if not dissipated properly, and your sofa can still smell like mala three days after cooking.

A closed concept kitchen provides more privacy, however, it can feel cramped. The choice depends on your lifestyle and how much you value an open space.

When it comes to an open versus a closed concept kitchen, it’s best not to follow trends blindly and instead speak to your ID to determine what works for your needs. They can suggest clever ways to achieve an open feel with glass partitions or a closed concept kitchen with spacious aisles. The options are endless!

‍

Lifestyle Considerations

‍

Hosting and Dining Preferences

When planning your kitchen renovation, think about how you live and cook. Do you frequently host dinner parties or prefer casual meals on the go? An open-concept kitchen is ideal for entertaining and keeping an eye on kids or guests. This layout promotes social interactions and allows for easy movement. Conversely, closed kitchens provide more privacy and can help contain cooking messes and odors, but they may feel cramped and isolated from the rest of the home.

‍

Kitchen Layout Choices

Choose a layout that matches your workflow. A U-shaped kitchen is efficient, with counters on three sides, creating an ideal work triangle for cooking. This layout provides ample counter space and storage but may feel too enclosed for some. A single-wall kitchen is compact and works well in smaller spaces, but it may require a kitchen island to provide additional workspace and storage. An L-shaped layout is space-efficient while allowing for some openness, making it a good compromise between the other two options.

‍

Daily Routine Considerations

Factor in your daily routines. If you start work early, consider incorporating an island with seating for a quick breakfast. This can serve as a convenient spot for morning coffee or quick meals. Avid bakers will want lots of counter space and storage for baking tools and ingredients. Frequent cooks need high-powered hobs, ovens, and ample fridge space to accommodate their culinary adventures. Casual cooks can get by with a basic range and a compact fridge, focusing more on convenience and efficiency.

‍

Countertop Height

Countertop height is another crucial factor to consider. Standard countertop height is typically around 90cm to a metre, which works well for most tasks and individuals. However, if you are taller or shorter than average, you might want to customise the height to reduce strain and improve comfort. For those who do a lot of baking, a lower countertop section can make rolling dough and other tasks easier. Consider your primary kitchen activities and choose a height that best suits your ergonomic needs.

‍

Types of Kitchen Layouts

The layout of your kitchen greatly impacts how efficiently you can cook and move around. The most common options for small kitchens in Singapore are the galley, L-shaped and U-shaped layouts.

‍

Galley Layout

The galley layout places cabinets and appliances on two opposite walls. This configuration is space-efficient, making it a popular choice for small kitchens in Singapore. However, it can sometimes feel cramped due to the narrow space between the two walls. This layout is best for solo cooking activities, as it doesn't allow much room for multiple people to work simultaneously.

‍

L-Shaped Layout

An L-shaped layout features cabinets on two adjoining walls, creating an open and spacious feel. This design provides a good balance of storage and countertop space, making it easier for multiple people to work comfortably in the kitchen. The L-shaped layout is ideal for those who want a more open and inviting kitchen environment while still maintaining functionality.

‍

U-Shaped Layout

For maximum storage and counter space, the U-shaped layout is the best option. Cabinets and appliances line three walls, creating an enclosed yet efficient workspace. This layout is perfect for those who prioritize having ample counter space and storage. However, it can feel too closed-in for some, so consider an open concept by removing upper cabinets on one wall to create a more open feel.

‍

Considerations for Small Kitchens

When planning the layout of a small kitchen in Singapore, it's essential to consider the balance between efficiency and space. Each of these layouts has its advantages and potential drawbacks, so choose the one that best suits your cooking style and space requirements. For an even more open and airy feel, consider integrating elements from open-concept designs where feasible.

‍

Kitchen Islands, Peninsulas and Bars

Kitchen islands are extremely popular and versatile in open concept kitchens. They provide additional counter space for food preparation and seating for casual dining. Peninsulas attached to a wall also work well for separating the kitchen from adjacent living areas. For smaller spaces, a kitchen bar or counter may be all you need.

Consider how you use your kitchen and the available floor area to determine what option suits you best. An island allows access from all sides and is ideal if you entertain often. A peninsula is good for defining zones in an open plan space. A bar or counter is perfect when space is limited but you still want a place to sit, eat and chat. The configuration you choose should complement your cooking workflow and lifestyle needs.

‍

Cabinets, Drawers and other Storage Solutions

Organising and maximising your kitchen storage space is key. For cabinets, go with either wood or laminate in a style you like. Drawers are a great option for easy access to utensils and gadgets. Mounted wall cabinets provide extra space without taking up floor space.

Pull-out drawers, turntables and pull-down racks help keep everything in sight and within reach. For ergonomics, keep frequently used items at counter height or in wall cabinets at eye level. Keep less used items higher or lower. Corner cabinets with rotating shelves avoid wasted space.

‍

Choosing the Right Materials

‍

Countertops

When it comes to countertops, the materials you choose can significantly impact both the aesthetics and functionality of your kitchen. Quartz and granite are two popular options due to their durability. Quartz is low-maintenance, requiring only regular cleaning, while granite needs annual sealing to maintain its appearance and resistance to stains. Both materials offer a range of colours and patterns, allowing you to find a perfect match for your kitchen design.

‍

Cabinets

For cabinets, consider the balance between cost and aesthetics. Plywood boxes with laminate or melamine fronts provide an affordable solution, ideal for budget-conscious renovations. For a more upscale look, opt for solid wood or lacquered MDF cabinets. These materials offer a richer, more luxurious finish and can be customised to fit your desired style and colour scheme.

‍

Backsplashes

Backsplashes are an essential design element that protects your walls and adds visual interest to your kitchen. Common materials include ceramic tile, stone, and glass, each offering unique textures and finishes. For a rustic or industrial feel, exposed brick or wood panels can be an excellent choice. The right backsplash can tie together your kitchen's overall design and add a touch of personality.

‍

Essential Appliances for Your New Kitchen

Once you’ve nailed down the layout and other design elements, it’s time to fill your kitchen up with the essential appliances!

‍

Stovetops

If you love to cook, your stovetop is one of the most critical appliances in your kitchen. Gas and induction stovetops are excellent options, each with unique benefits. Gas stovetops offer instant, controllable heat, making them ideal for high-heat cooking methods like searing and stir-frying. They provide superior temperature control compared to regular electric stovetops, giving you precise cooking results. Of course, we can’t forget about the wok hey that is only achievable with gas stoves.

Induction stovetops, on the other hand, are extremely energy-efficient and safe, as the cooking surface remains cool to the touch. Induction also offers superior temperature control, making it a great choice for those who prioritise safety and efficiency.

‍

Ovens

When it comes to ovens, consider the layout and functionality that best suits your cooking style. A wall oven paired with a separate cooktop offers flexibility and ergonomic benefits, while a range with an oven below can save space and streamline your kitchen design. Convection ovens are particularly beneficial as they circulate hot air, ensuring faster and more even cooking. Some modern ovens now offer both regular bake and convection options, providing versatility for various cooking techniques.

‍

Refrigerators

A good refrigerator is essential for keeping your ingredients fresh and your kitchen organised. Look for a refrigerator with ample storage to meet your needs, including adjustable shelves, humidity-controlled crisper drawers for produce, and temperature-controlled zones for different types of food. A unit with a bottom freezer drawer can be particularly convenient, as it allows for easier access to frequently used items without bending down. Additionally, consider features like water and ice dispensers for added convenience.

‍
